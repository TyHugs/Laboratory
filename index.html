Lessons Learned

Notes from the Lab

Principles earned the hard way — from building analytics infrastructure, shipping products, and navigating healthcare politics.

01

Ship First, Perfect Later

The best dashboard I ever built was the ugly one I shipped in 48 hours. Iteration beats perfection. Get it in front of users and let their feedback drive v2.

02

AI Is a Tool, Not a Strategy

The value comes from applying AI to specific, painful problems — not from the technology itself. Start with the pain point, then find the right model.

03

Data Quality Beats Model Complexity

Spent weeks on a sophisticated ML pipeline only to realize the real ROI was cleaning the input data. Garbage in, garbage out is a law.

04

Build for the Decision Maker

Nobody cares about your R² score. They care about whether to hire another surgeon. Translate analytics into decisions, not dashboards.

05

Automate the Boring Stuff

Every hour spent automating a manual report paid back tenfold. If you're doing it twice, script it. If you're doing it weekly, build a pipeline.

06

Your Network Is Your Net Worth

The best opportunities came from sharing work openly. A GitHub repo, a lunch conversation, a demo that impressed the right person at the right time.

About

The Person Behind the Lab

Tyler Hughes is a business strategist and analytics architect at the University of Michigan Health, where he serves as Lead Business Consultant for the Department of Urology.

He founded BizIQ, a comprehensive analytics platform now scaling across Michigan Medicine's surgical departments — and builds products ranging from patient-facing websites to AI-driven predictive models.

With an MBA and a background spanning technology sales, IT leadership, and healthcare operations, Tyler operates at the intersection of data, strategy, and execution. He believes the best way to learn is to build, and the best way to prove value is to ship.

Open to collaborations, consulting, and interesting problems.
