Operating Principles
01

Ship First, Perfect Later

The best dashboard I ever built was the ugly one I shipped in 48 hours. It was wrong in six places, but it was in the hands of the people who needed it. We fixed it together. That's how trust gets built.

02

Build for the Decision Maker

Nobody in a leadership meeting has ever asked about my R² score. They ask whether we should hire another surgeon, open a Saturday clinic, or renegotiate an OR block. Build for that question.

03

AI Is a Tool, Not a Strategy

I've seen teams buy a model and then search for a problem. Start with the pain point someone is losing sleep over. Then figure out whether the answer is AI, a spreadsheet, or a conversation.

04

Data Quality Beats Model Complexity

I spent three weeks building a predictive model before realizing the source data had duplicate provider entries across systems. The real ROI was in the cleanup. It always is.

05

Automate the Boring Stuff

If you're doing it twice, script it. If you're doing it weekly, build a pipeline. The hours I've saved on reporting automation funded every creative project on this page.

06

Share the Work Before It's Ready

The best opportunities I've had came from showing something unfinished to someone who could see its potential. Perfection is the enemy of connection.
